About

I am a passionate researcher with expertise in various advanced statistical methodologies. My research interests span several key areas within biostatistics, where I strive to uncover insights and develop innovative solutions for complex health-related problems. My primary research interests include causal inference, where I explore relationships and make sense of cause-effect patterns in data. I also focus on stochastic orderings in survival analysis, studying statistical methods to analyze time-to-event data. Additionally, I apply machine learning algorithms to extract insights and predict outcomes, enhancing our understanding of health trends and risks. Another area of my expertise is time series analysis, where I model and forecast temporal data for real-world applications, providing valuable predictions and insights. I am dedicated to advancing the field of biostatistics and contributing to impactful health research through these diverse and interconnected areas of study.
